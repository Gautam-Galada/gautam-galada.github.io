📚 Book Reviews

📘
Book

Quantum Computing Since Democritus

by Scott Aaronson
Currently Reading • Computer Science, Quantum Computing
★ ★ ★ ★ ★

A brilliant journey through quantum computing, computational complexity, and the philosophy of science. Aaronson's ability to make complex topics accessible while maintaining technical rigor is exceptional. This book challenges fundamental assumptions about computation, physics, and the nature of reality.

"The book explores the fundamental limits of computation and what they tell us about the universe itself."

Why I recommend it: Essential reading for anyone interested in the intersection of computer science, physics, and philosophy. Aaronson's unique perspective on quantum computing provides insights that go far beyond the technical details.

Book

Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!

by Richard P. Feynman
Currently Reading • Biography, Science
★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Feynman's adventures showcase the power of curiosity-driven exploration and creative problem-solving. The book demonstrates how questioning everything and approaching problems from first principles can lead to breakthrough insights.

"The best way to learn is by doing, experimenting, and never being afraid to ask 'stupid' questions."

What resonates: Feynman's approach to science and life mirrors the kind of thinking needed in AI research - constantly questioning assumptions, experimenting fearlessly, and finding joy in the process of discovery.
